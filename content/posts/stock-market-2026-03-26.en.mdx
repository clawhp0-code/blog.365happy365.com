## Today's Market in One Line

**"Panic selling in large-caps while KOSDAQ stands alone — a starkly polarized market"**

Today, the Korean stock market produced an unusual picture with KOSPI and KOSDAQ moving in opposite directions. While KOSPI crashed more than 3%, KOSDAQ actually closed higher, leaving investors bewildered.

---

## Index Trends

### KOSPI — 5,460.46 (-181.75, -3.22%)

KOSPI was pushed lower by heavy selling from the opening bell, with the decline widening steadily throughout the session. Simultaneous net selling from foreign and institutional investors weighed on the index, while top market-cap stocks like Samsung Electronics and SK hynix plunged in unison, amplifying downward pressure. The early breach of the 5,500 level further deteriorated investor sentiment.

### KOSDAQ — 1,136.64 (+15.20, +1.36%)

KOSDAQ showed a completely different trend from KOSPI. Retail investor buying flowed into biotech and secondary battery small/mid-cap stocks, maintaining steady gains throughout. The sentiment appeared to be driven by the logic that "KOSPI's excessive decline = KOSDAQ's relative attractiveness."

---

## Key Issues

- **U.S. Tariff Policy Uncertainty Resurfaces** — Ahead of the Trump administration's reciprocal tariff announcement, global risk-off sentiment intensified, hitting export-oriented large-caps in semiconductors and autos.
- **USD/KRW Exchange Rate Surges** — The exchange rate spiked to the upper 1,450-won range intraday, triggering foreign investor profit-taking.
- **Domestic Biotech Clinical Trial Catalyst** — Positive clinical trial results from some KOSDAQ biotech companies drove KOSDAQ's gains.

---

## Tomorrow's Outlook

In the short term, the **U.S. tariff announcement** is the biggest variable. If tougher-than-expected tariff measures are announced, further downside pressure is inevitable. Conversely, if there are signs of negotiation flexibility, a rebound attempt is possible. The KOSPI 5,400 level is drawing attention as short-term support, and with elevated volatility, careful position management is advised.
