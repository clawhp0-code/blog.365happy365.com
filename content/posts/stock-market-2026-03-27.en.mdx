## Today's Market in One Line

**"Peak concerns + external variables = a consolidation session"** — Both indices declined, with KOSDAQ's drop being particularly notable.

---

## Index Trends

| Index | Close | Change | % Change |
|-------|-------|--------|----------|
| KOSPI | 5,438.87 | -21.59 | -0.40% |
| KOSDAQ | 1,141.51 | -18.04 | -1.56% |

KOSPI opened in the flat range but selling intensified in the afternoon, ultimately closing below the 5,440 level. KOSDAQ saw heavy profit-taking centered on small/mid-cap growth stocks, with its decline roughly four times that of KOSPI. Foreign investors recorded slight net selling on KOSPI, while institutions maintained a neutral stance.

---

## Key Issues

- **U.S. Reciprocal Tariff Concerns Resurface** — The Trump administration's additional tariff mentions triggered global risk-off sentiment.
- **Strong USD/KRW Exchange Rate** — Continued dollar strength negatively impacted foreign investor flows.
- **Domestic Rate Hold Expectations vs. Inflation Pressure** — Bank of Korea monetary policy uncertainty heightened market volatility.
- **AI/Semiconductor Sector Correction** — Domestic related stocks weakened across the board following global big tech share price declines.

---

## Sector Performance

**Strong Sectors**
- Defense/Aerospace — Steady interest on continued geopolitical tensions
- Utilities (Electric/Gas) — Preference for defensive stocks
- Financials (Banks/Insurance) — Expectations of benefit from maintained rate levels

**Weak Sectors**
- Semiconductors/IT — Concentrated foreign selling, leading the index decline
- Secondary Batteries — Demand slowdown concerns reignited
- Bio/Healthcare — Major contributor to KOSDAQ's widened decline

## Tomorrow's Outlook

In the short term, whether **the 5,400 support level holds** is the key focal point. Depending on U.S. market closing results and month-end position adjustment flows, volatility could increase. KOSDAQ is expected to test the 1,130 support level, and without a clear catalyst, rebound potential may be limited. Rather than aggressive chasing, a strategy of **phased positioning and maintaining cash reserves** appears prudent.
