## What Is This?

You've had that experience at least once, right? Standing there staring at the night sky wondering, "Is that the Big Dipper? Or Cassiopeia?"

As of 2026, a gadget has arrived that completely eliminates that confusion. It's the **AI auto-tracking smart telescope 'Evestar Scope'**.

This telescope connects to your smartphone app via Bluetooth, and when you select a celestial object in the app, **the telescope automatically rotates to aim precisely at it**. Place it on the tripod, tap "Jupiter" in the app, and you're done. That's really all there is to it. No more complicated right ascension and declination calculations.

## Why Is This Special?

The biggest difference from existing smart telescopes is the **built-in AI star recognition engine**. When you open the app and point the camera at the sky, the AI analyzes the currently visible constellations in real time and labels them. It's like giving AR name tags to the stars.

Here's a summary of the key features:

- **Auto-Tracking**: Even as stars move due to Earth's rotation, the telescope automatically follows them
- **4600x optical zoom**: Clearly see lunar craters, Saturn's rings, and Jupiter's Great Red Spot
- **In-app real-time commentary**: Provides information and mythology about the celestial object being observed
- **Photo capture**: Take astrophotos directly with your smartphone and share on social media
- **Weight 1.2kg**: Fits in a backpack, usable at any campsite

The **"Today's Recommended Celestial Objects"** feature in the app is particularly impressive. Based on the season and your current location, it recommends the "Top 5 most visible celestial objects right now." Stunning night sky observation is possible even with zero astronomy knowledge.
