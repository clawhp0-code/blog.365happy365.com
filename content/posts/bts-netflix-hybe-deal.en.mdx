BTS is finally back as a full group! And they are holding an epic comeback live at Gwanghwamun Square, right in the heart of Seoul — the buzz is unreal.

But here is the interesting twist: this comeback show is not streaming on HYBE's own platform Weverse, but as a **Netflix exclusive**. At first, I thought, "Wait, why Netflix?" But looking deeper, there is a massive business strategy hiding beneath the surface. Today, let me break down how these two giants are achieving a win-win.

## Netflix: "All the ARMYs in the World, Come to Our House!"

For Netflix, this is an absolute goldmine. Tens of millions of ARMYs worldwide will flood to Netflix just to watch this single concert. In particular, their ad-supported tier subscriptions are expected to explode.

On top of that, Netflix has been aggressively eyeing live streaming, like sports broadcasts. If they can handle tens of millions of simultaneous connections without a single server crash while delivering flawless 4K quality, that is the perfect opportunity to flex their technical muscle for the global live streaming market.

## HYBE: "You Pay the Bills, We Keep the Tour Revenue!"

But honestly, the real genius here is HYBE. Imagine the astronomical cost of setting up that massive stage in the middle of Gwanghwamun, marketing it globally, and streaming it worldwide. **Netflix is footing the entire bill.** HYBE's cost burden is essentially zero.

What makes it even more impressive is that this exclusive deal covers **only this single Gwanghwamun concert**.

As everyone knows, BTS kicks off their real world tour starting in April, spanning 34 cities with 82 shows. All that massive tour revenue? HYBE keeps every penny — no splitting with Netflix.

In short, HYBE used Netflix as a global megaphone to broadcast "Our boys are back!" as the ultimate comeback trailer, without spending a dime. After watching the concert on Netflix and getting hyped, global fans are not going to sit still — they will rush to buy real concert tickets. It is a brilliantly clever move.

## "What If I Miss the Live Stream Because of Work?"

If you cannot catch the live broadcast because of real life (work, part-time jobs, classes...) or if you are overseas and the time zone does not work out, do not worry.

After the live broadcast wraps up, **it will be available as VOD (video on demand) on Netflix.** If you are already a Netflix subscriber, you can rewatch it in high quality anytime with no additional charge.
