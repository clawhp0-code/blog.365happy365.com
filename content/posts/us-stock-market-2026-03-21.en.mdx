## Today's US Stock Market — Broad Decline, an Uneasy Day

Today (March 21), US major indexes fell in unison, heightening investor tension. NASDAQ in particular recorded the largest decline, weighing on the broader market.

---

## Key Indexes at a Glance

**S&P 500** fell **-1.10%** to 6,551.91. Selling pressure was evenly distributed across the market, with no clear safe haven for the day.

**NASDAQ** dropped a significant **-1.61%**, sinking to the 21,796 level. Investor sentiment toward tech and growth stocks has frozen over.

**Dow Jones** and **Russell 2000** declined -0.77% and -0.74% respectively — relatively smaller drops, but neither escaped the red.

---

## What the VIX Tells Us

Today's most notable indicator is the **VIX (Fear Index)**. It surged **+5.69% to 25.43**. Generally, VIX above 20 is interpreted as a market anxiety signal. The current 25 level means investors are quite concerned about short-term volatility.

---

## Key Points for Investors

- **NASDAQ decline > Dow decline** — Time to review tech and growth-heavy portfolios
- **VIX breaks above 25** — Options market and hedging instruments are drawing increased interest
- **Russell 2000 modest decline** — Small and mid-caps held up relatively well, but it's too early to feel relieved

Overall, today's session had a clear **risk-off** tone. Rather than rushing to buy, this appears to be a time for a cautious, wait-and-see approach.
