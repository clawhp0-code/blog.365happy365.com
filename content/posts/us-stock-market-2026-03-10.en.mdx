## US Stock Market Recap, March 10, 2026: Broad-Based Decline

## Index-by-Index Analysis

**S&P 500** and **Dow Jones** both fell roughly 2%, reflecting clear selling pressure across the broader market. Even large caps couldn't mount a defense.

**NASDAQ** also declined 1.88%, which is relatively resilient for a tech-heavy index. However, a 427-point drop is far from trivial.

The most notable figure is the **Russell 2000's -3.30%** plunge. When small caps fall this hard, it's a signal that **risk-averse sentiment is strongly at work**.

## VIX (Fear Index) Surges

VIX **spiked 16.88% to 27.76**. Generally, VIX above 25 indicates the market is feeling considerable anxiety, and today it crossed that threshold. Preparation for expanded short-term volatility is necessary.

## Key Points for Investors

- **Small-cap plunge**: The Russell 2000's steepest decline suggests that economic slowdown concerns are being priced into the market.
- **VIX at 27**: If the fear index breaks 30, it enters panic territory, so watch for further increases.
- **Dollar-cost averaging approach**: Fear during sharp selloffs can turn into opportunity. However, since the decline could deepen, **gradual entry** is preferable to aggressive moves.

On days when the entire market drops like today, composure is a greater weapon than urgency. We'll keep watching tomorrow's market action!
