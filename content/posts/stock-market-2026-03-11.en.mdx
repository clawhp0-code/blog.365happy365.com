## Today's Market in One Line

> **"A breather after yesterday's explosion — KOSPI confirms support amid short-term correction"**

After surging more than 280 points the previous day, KOSPI pulled back today under profit-taking pressure. The consensus view is that this is a healthy cooldown from short-term overheating, and the broader trend itself remains intact.

---

## Index Trends

| Index | Close | Change | % Change |
|-------|-------|--------|----------|
| KOSPI | 5,489.73 | -42.86 | **-0.77%** |
| KOSDAQ | 1,121.34 | -16.34 | **-1.44%** |

Institutions took profits on the semiconductor and IT large-caps that led the previous day's rally, while KOSDAQ saw somewhat larger declines centered on small-cap growth stocks. Foreign investors maintained slight net buying, helping to prevent a sharp drop.

---

## Key Issues

- **U.S. Market Jitters Continue**: Major U.S. indices fell overnight, somewhat dampening global risk appetite.
- **Institutional Profit-Taking**: Selling pressure from institutions flowed into semiconductor and financial stocks that benefited from the previous day's surge.
- **USD/KRW Exchange Rate Rises Slightly**: A resumption of dollar strength had a neutral impact on foreign investor flows.
- **Technical Overbought Cooldown**: The market generally interprets this as a natural correction following RSI entering overbought territory.
