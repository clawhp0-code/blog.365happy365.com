## Today's Market in One Line

KOSPI plunges while KOSDAQ stands alone — a day when large-cap selling pressure hammered the market.

---

## Index Trends

### KOSPI: 5,763.22 (-161.81, -2.73%)
KOSPI plummeted **161.81 points (-2.73%)** today to close at 5,763.22. Simultaneous selling from foreign and institutional investors from the opening bell rapidly widened the decline, and no rebound momentum materialized through the close. Top market-cap stocks were broadly weak, dragging the index lower.

### KOSDAQ: 1,143.48 (+6.54, +0.58%)
In contrast, KOSDAQ gained **6.54 points (+0.58%)** to close at 1,143.48. Despite KOSPI's weakness, select small/mid-cap biotech and secondary battery stocks showed strength, supporting the index. Relative outperformance versus KOSPI was notable today.

---

## Key Issues

- **Renewed Global Tightening Concerns**: Market fears about the possibility of additional Fed rate hikes resurfaced, intensifying selling pressure across risk assets.
- **Massive Foreign Net Selling**: Foreign investors executed large-scale net selling in the KOSPI market, driving the index decline.
- **Strong USD/KRW Exchange Rate**: A continued strong dollar trend accelerated foreign capital outflows.

---

## Sector Performance

**Strong Sectors**
- Bio/Healthcare: Individual stock strength centered on KOSDAQ
- Defense: Some stocks rose on geopolitical risk concerns

**Weak Sectors**
- Semiconductors/IT: Concentrated foreign selling drove significant declines
- Auto/Secondary Battery Large-caps: Simultaneous institutional and foreign selling
- Financials/Banking: Broad weakness in rate-sensitive sectors

---

## Tomorrow's Outlook

In the short term, additional downward pressure on KOSPI may continue. Whether the 5,700 support level holds is critical, and close attention should be paid to overnight U.S. market trends and exchange rate direction. KOSDAQ may maintain its relative strength, but the possibility of a correlated decline cannot be ruled out if large-cap losses deepen. A wait-and-see approach appears more prudent than aggressive dip-buying.
