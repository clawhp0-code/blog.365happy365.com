## Today's Market in One Line

> **"Large-caps sold off while small/mid-cap growth stocks were bought"** — A day of stark decoupling between KOSPI and KOSDAQ!

---

## Index Trends

### KOSPI — 5,487.24 (-96.01, -1.72%)

KOSPI fell more than 96 points in a single day, giving up the 5,500 level. This was the largest single-day drop in the past month, driven by simultaneous selling from both foreign and institutional investors. The decline was concentrated in large-cap semiconductor and battery stocks at the top of the market cap rankings.

### KOSDAQ — 1,152.96 (+16.13, +1.42%)

In contrast, KOSDAQ saw strong dip-buying inflows and held above the 1,150 level. Retail investors actively bought small/mid-cap stocks in biotech, AI software, and secondary battery materials, with advancing stocks significantly outnumbering decliners.

---

## Key Issues

- **U.S. Macro Concerns**: The previous night's U.S. February CPI came in slightly above expectations, reigniting fears of a delayed Fed rate cut. This led to dollar strength and won weakness, putting pressure on foreign investor flows.
- **Semiconductor Export Regulation Concerns**: Foreign media reports about potential additional U.S. semiconductor export restrictions on China cooled sentiment around domestic semiconductor large-caps.
- **Biotech Catalyst**: A domestic biotech company announced positive Phase 2 FDA clinical trial results in the U.S., lifting the entire KOSDAQ biotech sector.

## Tomorrow's Outlook

- U.S. PPI release (local time March 13) will be digested before market open, potentially amplifying volatility depending on the results.
- The 5,450-5,480 range is drawing attention as short-term support for KOSPI; if this zone is breached, the possibility of further correction should be considered.
