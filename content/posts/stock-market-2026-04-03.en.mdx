## Today's Market Summary

**"Foreign buying reignites — KOSPI firmly above 5,300 and knocking on the door of 5,400!"**

Today's market was dominated by a resounding "buy" sentiment. The KOSPI climbed nearly 3%, displaying strong upward momentum, while the KOSDAQ joined the rally with a solid gain of over 1%.

---

## Index Performance

| Index | Close | Change | % Change |
|-------|-------|--------|----------|
| KOSPI | 5,377.30 | +143.25 | +2.74% |
| KOSDAQ | 1,063.75 | +11.36 | +1.08% |

The KOSPI saw strong buying pressure right from the opening bell, breezing past the 5,300 level with ease. It maintained its upward momentum through the afternoon session to close at 5,377.30. A single-day gain of 143 points is one of the most notable moves seen in recent months. The KOSDAQ's gains were more modest compared to the KOSPI, but its recovery above the 1,063 level kept the overall tone positive.

---

## Key Drivers

- **Wall Street tailwinds**: The Nasdaq and S&P 500 both posted strong gains in the previous session in New York, boosting risk appetite among investors.
- **Foreign buying resumes**: Foreign investors returned as net buyers in force, driving the index higher with concentrated purchases in large-cap semiconductor stocks such as Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix.
- **KRW/USD exchange rate stabilizes**: A slight decline in the exchange rate (Korean won strengthening) created a favorable environment for continued foreign capital inflows.

## Outlook for Tomorrow

Following today's sharp rally, it's worth assessing whether the market is entering short-term overbought territory. Selling pressure may emerge around the 5,400 level, and volatility could pick up depending on the outcome of the U.S. nonfarm payrolls report. Rather than chasing the rally, a **phased buying on pullbacks** strategy appears to be the more prudent approach.
