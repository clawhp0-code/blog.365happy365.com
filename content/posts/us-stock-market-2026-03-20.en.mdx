## US Markets Close Sharply Lower Across the Board

On March 20, 2026, all major US indexes fell sharply, raising investor concerns.

## Index-by-Index Breakdown

**S&P 500** closed at 6,579.24, dropping **2.04%** in a single day. A decline of this magnitude in the benchmark index is a signal that shouldn't be taken lightly.

**NASDAQ** slid over **511 points for a 2.27% decline**, reflecting intense selling in tech stocks. The weakening sentiment toward growth stocks is clearly visible.

**Dow Jones** recorded the steepest drop among the three major indexes, plunging **1,153 points (-2.45%)**. Even traditional large caps were shaken, indicating pressure across the entire market.

The small and mid-cap gauge **Russell 2000** also fell **2.26%**, with no exceptions to the downturn.

## VIX: Anxiety Persists

**VIX rose slightly to 25.27**. Since readings above 20 are typically interpreted as a market anxiety signal, investor sentiment is significantly dampened.

## Key Points for Investors

- A **synchronized decline across all indexes** may signal that macro-level risk factors are at play, rather than sector-specific issues
- **VIX above 25** indicates a high short-term volatility zone, so leveraged positions require extra caution
- In times like these, **dollar-cost averaging** and **portfolio reviews** can be the wisest responses
