## Today's Market in One Line

**"Large caps soared while small caps took a rest"** — KOSPI rallied on its own as KOSDAQ paused to catch its breath.

---

## Index Performance

### KOSPI 📈
- **Closing Price:** 5,450.33pt
- **Change:** +73.03pt (+1.36%)

The KOSPI maintained a steady upward trajectory from the opening bell, driven by simultaneous buying from both foreign and institutional investors. Export-oriented large caps — particularly semiconductors and automakers — led the charge, firmly anchoring the index above the 5,450 level. Foreign investors recorded net purchases of approximately 420 billion KRW, extending their buying streak to three consecutive sessions.

### KOSDAQ 📉
- **Closing Price:** 1,047.37pt
- **Change:** -8.97pt (-0.85%)

The KOSDAQ failed to hold the 1,050 level, closing modestly lower. The decline is largely attributed to concentrated profit-taking following a strong recent rebound. Decliners were concentrated in small- and mid-cap biotech and secondary battery stocks, with retail investor selling pressure driving the move downward.

---

## Key Issues

1. **Anticipation of Fed Minutes Release** — Ahead of this week's scheduled FOMC minutes release, growing expectations for a resumption of the rate-cutting cycle translated into foreign buying activity.
2. **USD/KRW Exchange Rate Stabilizes** — The won held steady in the early 1,320s range intraday, creating a favorable environment for export-driven stocks.
3. **Samsung Electronics HBM Order Hopes** — The possibility of an HBM4 supply contract with a major AI chip manufacturer had a positive ripple effect across the market.

## Outlook for Tomorrow

In the near term, whether the KOSPI can consolidate above the 5,450 level will be the key variable to watch. It will be important to monitor both the U.S. market close and broader Asian market trends. For the KOSDAQ, keep a close eye on whether it can recover the 1,050 level. **Given that profit-taking pressure remains present, it would be wise to stay prepared for the possibility of heightened short-term volatility.**
