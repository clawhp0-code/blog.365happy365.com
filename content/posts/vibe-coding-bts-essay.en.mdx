These days, my head is filled with nothing but thoughts of 'vibe coding.' Going back and forth with AI to produce the results I want is quite fun, but after spending an entire day in front of the monitor — refining prompts and piecing together logic — my brain starts to feel like it's short-circuiting from overload.

Tonight, I hit that wall: "Okay, I absolutely cannot keep going." I quietly lifted my hands from the keyboard.

Dragging my exhausted body to the sofa, I sank deep into the cushions and mindlessly turned on Netflix. As it happened, the screen was streaming BTS's Gwanghwamun comeback live. Dazzling lights filling the heart of Seoul, an explosion of cheers, and the members dancing like the stage might break apart.

Wow — what on earth is that incredible energy?

It felt like a completely different world from the one I'd just been living in, wrestling with text under the cold glow of a monitor. As I sat there in a daze, letting the bass pound through my chest and the scorching heat reach me through the screen, the tangled mess in my head cleared right out. Nothing beats a powerful sensory jolt when it comes to giving the brain a proper rest.

When I think about it, it's really quite interesting. What I've been immersed in lately is a kind of 'vibe' too — moving beyond rigid syntax to find a flow with AI. But that energy exploding on stage, breathing together with tens of thousands of fans — that's the real, absolute 'vibe.' Whether it's in front of a monitor or on a dazzling stage, the way people lose themselves in something and ride that wave is strangely similar.

This time spent mindlessly absorbed in music and performance — it's been the first proper brain reset I've had in a long while.

Having soaked up all the heat from that electrifying stage, I think I'll sleep deeply and soundly tonight. I have a good feeling that tomorrow morning, my fingers will feel lighter, and I'll be riding my own coding vibe with renewed energy.
